# Format Add-On: LinkedIn Post

Layer this on top of the master style guide for LinkedIn feed posts.

## Length

Three short paragraphs MAX. Anything longer belongs in a LinkedIn article (different format, different distribution).

## The "see more" cutoff

LinkedIn truncates the post at roughly the first 210 characters on desktop, less on mobile. The hook line plus one supporting sentence must land before the cutoff or readers never click "see more."

- **Line 1 (hook):** Conflict & Resolution opening, compressed to one sentence.
- **Line 2 (the turn):** A second sentence that earns the click. Concrete detail, surprise, or stake.

## Structure

1. **Hook block (~210 chars):** Lines 1 and 2 above. No emoji here. The first emoji on the page is a recruiter-detector flag.
2. **Body (1-2 paragraphs):** Specifics. Numbers, names, tools, time savings. One memorable phrase or analogy if it fits.
3. **CTA line:** Single short sentence. If linking to anything off-platform, the link goes in the FIRST COMMENT, never the body. LinkedIn's algorithm tanks reach on body links.

## Required elements

- Hook lands inside the truncation window.
- One concrete number, time savings, or named tool.
- 1-3 hashtags maximum, all at the end on a single line.

## Forbidden in LinkedIn format

- Body links (use first comment).
- "Broetry" formatting (one sentence per line, blank line between every line).
- Hashtag walls (5+ tags).
- Engagement-bait CTAs ("Agree?", "Thoughts?", "What would you add?").
- Tagging more than three people in the post itself.
- Em-dash-as-stylistic-flourish (it screams AI on this platform especially).
