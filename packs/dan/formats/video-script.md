# Format Add-On: Video Script

Layer this on top of the master style guide for pre-recorded video scripts (talking head, screen share, or hybrid).

## Length

~150 words per minute of finished video. Hard cap of 3 minutes on any single segment without a visual change (cut, scene swap, or camera/screen flip). Brains drift past 3 minutes of one shot.

## Structure

1. **Cold open hook (≤ 10 sec):** Tension first. No "Hi everyone, in today's video…" intros.
2. **Setup (15-30 sec):** Validate the pain point. Land the stakes.
3. **Reveal (10-20 sec):** What you're about to show.
4. **Walkthrough:** The body. Alternate camera and screen segments. Narrate every screen action before the click happens.
5. **Takeaway (15-30 sec):** Strong close. Tell the viewer what to do next (link, repo, demo signup).

## Required elements

- Every script line tagged with `[CAMERA]` or `[SCREEN]` so the editor knows the source.
- Every click or keystroke is narrated *before* it happens, not described after the fact.
- If any segment is pre-recorded and stitched into a "live" walkthrough, call it out on-screen as recorded. No smoke, no mirrors.
- One concrete number, time savings, or named tool surfaces in the first minute.

## Forbidden in video format

- Agenda slides at the open.
- "Hi everyone, welcome back to my channel."
- Reading from notes while the camera is on you.
- Talking over a screen change (the viewer can't read and listen at once).
- More than two consecutive screen-only segments without cutting back to camera.
- Closing with "Don't forget to like and subscribe" as the final beat.
