# Sample: "Building a Solid Foundation: The Role of the Build Command in Tanzu Platform"

**Source:** [blogs.vmware.com/tanzu](https://blogs.vmware.com/tanzu/unlocking-the-golden-commands-part1/) (Sep 6, 2024)

## Verbatim Excerpts

> As the saying goes, "The strength of a structure lies in its foundation." If the goal involves moving code to production, compiling and packaging code in a repeatable and secure manner is core to that objective.

> Dockerfiles seem simple and straightforward, and in fact, they are… in the beginning. However, Dockerfiles are like pigs—individually, they can be cute and manageable, but when you have lots of them, things quickly become a muddy mess.

## What this demonstrates

- **Maker mindset (Section 5):** "Solid Foundation," "structure," "foundation". Construction metaphor in title and lead.
- **Memorable analogy:** Dockerfiles-as-pigs is distinctly Dan. Concrete, slightly irreverent, sticks in the reader's head.
- **Golden Command Structure (Principle D):** This piece kicks off the "Unlocking the Golden Commands" series framing four verbs: Build, Bind, Deploy, Scale.
- **"Not a Science Project" stance (Principle E):** The pigs metaphor frames DIY container management as a failure mode.

## Style-guide compliance (pre-v3.0)

The verbatim quote contains an em dash. Banned in v3.0. The quote also uses "However" as a sentence opener, discouraged in Section 1, Rule 4.

The voice is on-brand even where the formal vocabulary discipline isn't enforced. These rules are exactly what v3.0 tightens.
