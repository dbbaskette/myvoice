# Sample: "The Best Place to Run AI Apps is Tanzu Platform"

**Source:** [blogs.vmware.com/tanzu](https://blogs.vmware.com/tanzu/the-best-place-to-run-ai-apps-is-tanzu-platform/)

## Verbatim Opening

> Since its original inception as Pivotal Cloud Foundry, Tanzu Platform's mission of helping enterprise customers build and deliver *great software quickly*, has not changed. Through the years *great software* has included everything from cloud native and 12 factor apps to microservices and functions to mobile apps, moduliths and software running at the edge. Today the list includes GenAI and agentic AI.
>
> To quote a famous Pivotal Cloud Foundry Haiku written by Onsi Fakhouri:
>
> *Here is my source code*
> *Run it in the cloud for me*
> *I do not care how*

## Body excerpt

> Platforms not only help you go fast, they allow you to do so consistently and at scale.

## What this demonstrates

- **The "Better Together" motif (Principle C):** Frames Tanzu and AI as continuation of a long pattern, not a new product silo.
- **Insider quote as shorthand:** The Onsi haiku does the value-prop work in three lines. Community lore beats a paragraph of pitch.
- **Speed to Value vocabulary (Principle B):** "go fast, consistently, at scale". Outcomes over features.
- **The Full Arc (Section 4):** Connects code to cloud to today's GenAI without breaking the through-line.

## Style-guide compliance (pre-v3.0)

The full body of the source post contains two banished words from Section 1, Rule 2 (one verb, one adjective). The voice is on-brand. Vocabulary discipline is the gap v3.0 closes.
