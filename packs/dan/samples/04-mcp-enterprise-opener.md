## Verbatim Opening

> In Part 1 of this blog series, I laid out why database access is the hardest MCP problem and explored the security patterns every implementation needs to get right. I used gp-mcp-server, an open source prototype I built for Greenplum and PostgreSQL, to make those patterns concrete. (If you haven't read it yet, start there.)
>
> This article picks up where that one left off. The prototype proved the patterns. Now I want to show you what those patterns look like at enterprise scale.

## Body excerpt: Golden Command in action

> [The server] incorporates more than 30 purpose-built tools organized around a deliberate workflow: **Discover the Schema. Constrain the Query. Contain the Data. Instrument the System.**

## What this demonstrates

- **The Golden Command Structure (Principle D):** Four capitalized verbs make a 30-tool surface feel manageable.
- **Maker advocacy (Section 1):** "an open source prototype I built" establishes builder credibility immediately, with no resume preamble.
- **Plain conversational handoff:** "Now I want to show you…" is a direct setup with no fluff opener and no "In this post we will explore…"
- **The Full Arc (Section 4):** Calls the previous post by name, then sets expectations for this one in two short sentences.
